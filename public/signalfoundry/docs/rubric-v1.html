Verity Scan Rubric v1

Definitions, scoring, and delivery gates.

Rubric v1 is the customer-safe scoring model for Verity Scans. It measures observable website clarity and readiness. It does not measure rankings, traffic, leads, conversions, backlinks, Google Business Profile performance, or placement in AI answers unless those surfaces are explicitly added to a future scan version.

Do not lower scores for sales urgency. Adjust scores only when retained evidence and the rubric support it.

Overall formula

Business Clarity25%
Search Readiness20%
AI Readiness20%
Trust Signals20%
Content Coverage15%

Overall score = Business Clarity × 0.25 + Search Readiness × 0.20 + AI Readiness × 0.20 + Trust Signals × 0.20 + Content Coverage × 0.15. Round once after calculating the weighted score.

Dimension definitions

Business Clarity

Business identity, primary offer, intended audience, geography/service area, contact path, and cross-page consistency.

Search Readiness

Crawl access, metadata/indexability, identity/location consistency, appropriate business schema, service-page coverage, and internal discovery paths.

AI Readiness

Entity/schema specificity, structured service meaning, answer-ready content, factual consistency, authorship/provenance, and machine accessibility.

Trust Signals

Verifiable contact/location, people and credentials, reviews/testimonials, outcomes, affiliations/awards, process/policies, and third-party corroboration.

Content Coverage

Core services, customer decision questions, process/next steps, cost expectations, geography, audience scenarios, and ownership/recency.

Evidence states

StateMeaning
passChecked and fully supported by retained evidence.
partialChecked and partly supported. Partial credit must use an explicit 25%, 50%, or 75% anchor.
failChecked and absent; zero points.
not_applicableRemoved from denominator and remaining weights normalized.
unknownNot enough evidence; never treated as pass or fail.

No evidence means no defensible points. Fetch failure creates unknown, not fail.

Publishability gates

  • Do not publish a dimension score unless at least 80% of applicable indicator weight was measured.
  • Every critical indicator must be checked.
  • Primary evidence must be retained internally.
  • Low-confidence dimensions do not get numeric customer scores.
  • Final customer reports require named human approval, live-site comparison, and customer-report comparison.

Internal report requirements

  • Rubric version and score snapshot.
  • Dimension and indicator calculations.
  • Measured-weight coverage and confidence.
  • Exact evidence and source URL for every indicator.
  • Manual reviewer state and delivery-gate status.
  • Current/proposed schema and implementation notes when relevant.
  • Customer-safe explanation used externally.
